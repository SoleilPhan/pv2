Symbio

Symbio is a web application that allows the Sales Representatives of BNY Mellon to have constant access to updates through custom filters. The potential knowledge gained from these updates will offer the sales team a cutting-edge advantage over their competitors.

Research

The goal of this project was to empower the Global Markets department's business decisions within BNY Mellon. I, along with Matthew Hsieh, Cristina Shin, and So-Hee Woo, conducted extensive user research by using a participatory make tool as well as interviews that allowed us to develop a concept map of the roles within the department. After the research phase, our key takeaway was to get more granular information- location, frequency, and source of where the data was found.

Persona

For the final persona, we decided to focus on one main user: the sales representative. It was clear that they were the ones on the client-facing side who also had the most ground in maintaining and developing relationships. Once we chose the sales representative, we created a scenario that used our product prior to any client meeting they may have.

Final Design

Sales representatives can add a client by entering their company name, add a line of business, and set specific filters that create a customized client dashboard. If a sales representative has multiple clients, they can easily manage them by creating multiple profiles.



Getting to a more granular level, users can search relevant documents based on needs.





If sales representatives want to visualize the location from where the data originated, they can do so with the geographic representation of each resource. Users can see the connections from the topic source to topic location with an open circle representing external resources and a closed circle representing internal resource.

Prototype



We decided to use HTML5 technology to allow for cross platform usage. As an open-source project, our source code can be found on Github.
